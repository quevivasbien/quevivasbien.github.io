Safety coalitions in risky innovation

What is this?

This applet simulates a game where two or more players compete to develop a risky technology. The players want to be the first to create the technology but have to make a tradeoff between speed and safety. Each player has two strategies available:

  • Baseline strategy: The player focuses on creating the technology as soon as possible.
  • Safe strategy: The player proceeds more carefully, reducing risk but potentially taking longer to create the technology.

In short, the baseline strategy is high performance but high risk, while the safe strategy is low performance but low risk.

The first player to create the technology gets a payoff of 1. However, depending on their strategy, they may cause a disaster that hurts all the players.

Finally, the players who play the safe strategy can form a coalition: they agree to share resources (e.g. intellectual property) which boasts their performance. The performance boast scales with the number of players who play the safe strategy.

You can adjust the parameters of the game and run the simulation to see how the players' strategies affect the outcome. The result shown is the best possible correlated equilibrium for the game.
